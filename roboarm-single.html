What is the Robo Arm?

The Robo Arm is a cutting-edge computer gadget designed to interact with your keyboard. By automating keystrokes, it offers an alternative way to engage with a computer.

Scripted Interactions

Have a set of commands you often repeat? Program the Robo Arm with a predefined script and watch it execute precise keystrokes at specific timings.

Automate tasks, run sequences, or simply have fun watching it play out your favorite keyboard shortcuts.

Voice-Activated Commands

In a rush or just prefer a hands-free approach? Just say the word! With our voice recognition, you can command the Robo Arm to tap specific keys with pinpoint accuracy.

Whether you're instructing a series of commands or calling for a single keystroke, the Robo Arm listens and acts.

Enhanced Positioning with Rotary Encoder

We've integrated a rotary encoder into the Robo Arm. This ensures precise and high-quality positioning right under each key, giving you a more accurate and reliable interaction every time.

Affordable by Design

Built using cost-effective components like Arduino, servo motors, rotary encoders, and 3D printed parts. Ideal for individuals and educational institutions like schools and lyceums.

Customizable to Your Needs

We provide 3D models of the Robo Arm components. Customize, modify, or expand the design to better suit your individual requirements for a truly personalized experience.

Open Source Commitment

Everything is open source. Enthusiasts, educators, and innovators worldwide can modify, improve, and share their versions, ensuring it remains affordable and accessible for everyone.

The Bigger Picture

While the Robo Arm was conceived from a personal observation, its potential extends far beyond. Whether you're looking for a different way to interact with your computer or seeking solutions for specific challenges, the Robo Arm is here to assist.

By supporting this open source project, you're endorsing a vision of inclusive computing.

Components You'll Need

You will need to purchase the following items:

Electronics:

  • • 1x Arduino Nano
  • • 1x PCA9685 16-channel servo motor board
  • • 4x SG90 or MG90S servo motors (180°)
  • • 1x USB 2.0 power adapter 5V 1-2A (to power supply PCA9685 board)
  • • Necessary wires (check wiring folder in documentation)
  • • Additional wires for two of the motors
  • • USB cable to connect Arduino Nano to Windows PC

Mechanical:

  • • 1x Bearing 35x47x7mm (d=35mm, D=47mm, B=7mm)
  • • 3D printed parts (STL files provided in file storage)

Setup Requirements:

Connect Arduino Nano to Windows 10 or 11 PC with installed Arduino drivers. Once connected, you should see it in the Device Manager.

Estimated cost: $30-50 USD
Components available on Amazon, AliExpress, or local electronics stores
